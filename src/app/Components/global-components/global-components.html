The Green House Effect

The greenhouse effect is the process where Earth’s atmosphere traps heat, similar to how glass traps heat in a greenhouse. Sunlight passes through the atmosphere and warms Earth’s surface. Some of this heat escapes into space, while the rest is absorbed by greenhouse gases in the troposphere, warming the air. The main greenhouse gases are water vapor, carbon dioxide, chlorofluorocarbons, methane, and nitrous oxide, with water vapor and carbon dioxide responsible for most heat absorption.
